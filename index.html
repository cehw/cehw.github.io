About

Current Role: Postdoctoral Fellow, HKUST Education: Ph.D. HKUST (2025) · BSc HHU (2021) Core Focus: Scientific ML for urban climate

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in Civil and Environmental Engineering at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), advised by Prof. Hui Su. My research develops scientific machine learning methods for urban microclimate prediction and atmospheric applications.

I also earned my Ph.D. from the HKUST Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering, advised by Prof. Jiachuan Yang.

My recent work combines physical understanding with data-driven modeling, with an emphasis on graph neural networks and multimodal approaches for short-term urban air temperature forecasting.

I believe AI will reshape both the way society works and the way science is conducted. Outside research, I enjoy natural landscapes, hiking, photography, and spending time by the sea.

Research Focus

Scientific ML

Physics-aware deep learning for robust prediction under limited observations and changing atmospheric conditions.

Urban Climate

Modeling canopy-layer temperature dynamics and the impact of urban form, vegetation, and synoptic forcing.

Spatiotemporal Forecasting

Multi-scale, graph-based, and multimodal methods for high-resolution city-scale nowcasting and short-term forecasting.
